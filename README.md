# NeuroWeave

**Coordination Infrastructure for Intelligent Systems**

NeuroWeave is an open-source coordination layer designed for intelligent systems, AI agents, automation platforms, and distributed decision-making environments.

As AI systems evolve beyond single-model interactions, coordination becomes the new bottle neck.

Agents need to communicate.
Workflows need to adapt.
Tools need to cooperate.
Decisions need context.

NeuroWeave exists to provide the infrastructure that enables intelligent systems to work together reliably at scale.

---

## Why NeuroWeave Exists

Modern AI systems are no longer isolated applications.

Today's environments contain:

* Multi-Agent Systems
* Autonomous Workflows
* Tool-Calling Agents
* Event-Driven Architectures
* Distributed Reasoning Networks
* Human-in-the-Loop Processes

Yet most systems still rely on ad-hoc coordination patterns.

The result:

* Fragmented communication
* Lost context
* Duplicate reasoning
* Workflow bottlenecks
* Poor fault tolerance
* Difficult debugging

NeuroWeave aims to solve these challenges through a unified coordination layer.

---

## Vision

Build an open coordination standard for intelligent systems.

A future where agents, tools, workflows, and services can cooperate through a shared infrastructure instead of isolated implementations.

---

## Core Principles

### Coordination First

Intelligence is valuable.
Coordinated intelligence is transformative.

### Open By Design

Protocols, specifications, and implementations should be transparent and community-driven.

### Modular Architecture

Every component should be replaceable, extensible, and interoperable.

### Human + AI Collaboration

Humans remain a critical part of complex decision systems.

NeuroWeave is designed to support both autonomous and assisted workflows.

---

## Potential Capabilities

### Agent Communication

Structured communication channels between intelligent agents.

### Task Orchestration

Dynamic assignment, delegation, and execution of tasks across distributed systems.

### Context Sharing

Secure and efficient exchange of memory, state, and execution context.

### Workflow Coordination

Synchronization of multi-step processes across agents, tools, and services.

### Failure Recovery

Resilient coordination patterns for handling interruptions, retries, and degraded systems.

### Observability Integration

Visibility into decisions, communication paths, and execution flows.
